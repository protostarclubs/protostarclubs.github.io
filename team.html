Our Team.

We are a community of students who enjoy making things, enjoy STEAM in abundance, and believe in entrepreneurship and the youth as the key to solving problems plaguing our society today. Dedicated, self-driven, and absurdly passionate - introducing the Protostar team:

Yamilett Estrada-Reyes

Co-Founder & CEO

Yamilett is a sophomore at Lane Tech College Prep, and an advocate for greater inclusivity in STEM. Driven by an unquenchable curiosity and intrinsic passion for social good, she leverages the intersection between CS + entrepreneurship in order to inflict change within her community. Outside of Protostar, she leads CS workshops at local middle schools, organizes hackathons, and is heavily involved in all-female or all-Latinx CS communities as an NCWIT Awardee, Stanford SHE++ Fellow, and Techqueria member. During her free time, she likes to workout at her local boxing gym, explore local coffee shops, and attend hackathons with her friends. Through her experiences, she hopes to close the gender and racial gap in STEM and inspire Latinas and other first-generation Americans, like herself, to pursue careers in STEM.

Tia Hawthorne

Co-Founder & COO

Tia Hawthorne is a sophomore at Lane Tech College Prep, and is the COO and Co-Founder of Protostar. With a heavy interest in business and tech, Tia has immersed herself in multiple areas of both industries, as she is also the Co-Founder of Power By Girls: an organization based aimed towards inspiring and empowering the next wave of female change makers and innovators, and the Illinois State Head of Elevate the Future: a non-profit organization which teaches elementary school students about the world(s) of business and tech. In her free time, she takes classes online as well as at the University of Chicago, and Tia is also an avid member of the GirlsWhoCode chapter at Lane Tech and of the Junior Economic Club of Chicago.

Noah Jillson

Co-Founder & CMO

Noah Jillson is a junior at Lane Tech College Prep, and is the Chief Marketing Officer and Co-Founder of Protostar. He has always been interested in creative problem solving which has led him to computer science, engineering, and eventually, entrepreneurship. He has a passion for helping other high school students learn and develop their entrepreneurial and problem solving skills and is doing so through Protostar. Outside of Protostar, he is an avid cyclist and was one of the founding members on a team that built and programmed an autonomous vehicle for a competition at Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology.
